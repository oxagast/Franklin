The Jewish Bible, or Tanakh, is the collection of scripture that is considered to be the authoritative word of God by Jews. It includes the Torah (the five books of Moses), the Nevi'im (the Prophets), and the Ketuvim (the Writings). The Tanakh is composed of a variety of genres including narrative, poetry, wisdom, and prophecy. These genres often include fantasy, metaphor, and allegory to convey the truth of the stories and teachings.
