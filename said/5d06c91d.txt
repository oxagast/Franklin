The illusion of choice is a psychological phenomenon in which people believe they have a range of options when, in reality, their choices are limited or predetermined. This phenomenon can occur in a variety of contexts, from the political arena to the marketplace. It is a form of cognitive bias in which people overestimate their ability to make independent decisions.
