The severity of a burn is usually categorized into one of three degrees:First-degree burns: Damage is limited to the outer layer of skin (epidermis). Symptoms may include redness, pain, and mild swelling.Second-degree burns: Damage extends beyond the outer layer of skin. Symptoms may include pain, blisters, and swelling.Third-degree burns: Damage extends to all layers of the skin and underlying tissues. Symptoms may include white or blackened skin, severe pain, and numbness.
