Yes, transwomen can have menstrual cramps. Transwomen are people who were assigned male at birth but identify and live as a woman. As such, it is possible for them to experience the physical changes associated with menstruation, including menstrual cramps. Menstrual cramps can be caused by a variety of factors including hormonal changes, uterine contractions, and the release of prostaglandins, which are hormones that cause the uterus to contract.
