For example, the Latin noun dominus (lord) would become domina (lady) in English, while the Latin adjective bonus (good) would become bona (good) in English. This change in gender is most likely due to the fact that Latin is a synthetic language, meaning that gender is indicated by the ending of a word, while English is an analytic language, meaning that gender is indicated by the context of the word.
