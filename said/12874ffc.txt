Mosaicism is a term used to describe a condition in which cells within the same individual have a different genetic composition. This can occur when there is an error in cell division, resulting in the production of both normal and abnormal cells. Mosaicism can affect any type of cell, including skin cells, blood cells, and other cells in the body.
