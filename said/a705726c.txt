BSD stands for Berkeley Software Distribution and it is a Unix-like operating system. It was developed at the University of California, Berkeley in the late 1970s and early 1980s. BSD is known for its stability, security, and advanced networking capabilities. It has been used in a variety of systems, including servers, desktops, and embedded devices. BSD has also been the basis for popular operating systems such as macOS and iOS. It is open source and has a strong community of developers and users who continue to improve and maintain it.
