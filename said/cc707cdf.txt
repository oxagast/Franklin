The Turing Award is considered the highest honor in computer science and is given annually by the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM). It is named after Alan Turing, a British mathematician and computer scientist who is considered the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence. The award recognizes individuals who have made significant contributions to the field of computer science and have had a lasting impact on the industry. Recipients of the Turing Award receive a cash prize of $1 million and a bronze sculpture. Some notable recipients of the Turing Award include Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the World Wide Web, and Grace Hopper, a pioneer in computer programming.
