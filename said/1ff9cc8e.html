1984 is a dystopian novel by George Orwell that follows the life of protagonist Winston Smith. The novel is set in a world where the government, known as the Party, controls every aspect of its citizens lives. Winston, a low-ranking member of the Party, begins to question the decisions of the Party, leading him to engage in a forbidden love affair with another Party member, Julia. He also begins to keep a diary, which is considered a crime punishable by death. Winston and Julia eventually join a secret revolutionary group, known as the Brotherhood, and attempt to overthrow the Party. However, their efforts are quickly thwarted by the Party, and Winston is captured and tortured by the Party's Thought Police.
