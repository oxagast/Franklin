1. Ad Hominem: Arguing against an individual instead of the issue at hand.

2. False Dilemma: Presenting two options that are not mutually exclusive.

3. Slippery Slope: Assuming a chain reaction of events will occur when there is no logical evidence to support it.

4. Straw Man: Distorting an opponent's argument to make it easier to refute.

5. Appeal to Authority: Assuming that because an authority figure says something, it must be true.

6. Hasty Generalization: Drawing a conclusion based on too little evidence.

7. Circular Argument: Restating the same point in different words to form a conclusion.

8. Red Herring: Introducing irrelevant topics to distract from the main point.

9. Bandwagon: Assuming that an opinion is correct just because it is popular.

10. False Analogy: Comparing two unrelated things and drawing a conclusion based on the comparison.
