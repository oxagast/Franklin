This thesis is an exploration of the cultural implications of chocolate death, a phenomenon in which people form emotional connections with chocolate and become unable to reduce or stop their chocolate consumption. It examines the psychological, social, and economic factors that contribute to chocolate death, as well as the ways in which it affects individuals, families, and communities. It looks at the potential for prevention and treatment of chocolate death, and ultimately considers the importance of a holistic approach to understanding the issue.
