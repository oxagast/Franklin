The Anglos and the Saxons were two early Germanic tribes that inhabited Britain during the 5th and 6th centuries. The Anglos migrated from northern Germany and Denmark and settled in East Anglia, Kent, and parts of what are now known as Northumbria and Mercia. The Saxons came from northwest Germany and the Netherlands and settled in Sussex, Essex, and Wessex.
