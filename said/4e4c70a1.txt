Baroque music is characterized by its use of complex polyphony, contrapuntal textures, and the use of ornamentation and improvisation. Neoclassicism was a movement in the early 20th century that focused on structure and order. This style of music is more linear, and features clear melodies and a regular pulse. It is often simpler and more direct than Baroque music.
