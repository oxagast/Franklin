Napalm is created when gasoline is combined with an agent that makes it stickier and more adhesive. One of the most common agents used is sugar, which is dissolved in the gasoline. The sugar helps to create a viscous, syrupy mixture that clings to surfaces. When ignited, the fuel mixture burns slowly and sticks to whatever it was applied to, making it an effective weapon.
