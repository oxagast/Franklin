bookworm asked:
     explain rtl embeddings in Unicode

.

RTL embeddings in Unicode are characters which are encoded to render text from right-to-left (RTL). This allows for more accurate representation of text in languages that are written from right-to-left, such as Arabic and Hebrew. RTL embeddings are especially useful for ensuring that the rendered text is properly ordered and displayed, as well as for enabling bidirectional and mixed-directional text.
