Yes, this is a phenomenon known as the Flynn effect. Research has revealed that average IQ scores have been steadily increasing since the mid-twentieth century, but in some countries, the rate of increase has slowed or even reversed. This can be attributed to a variety of factors, such as changes in educational systems, changes in the labor market, and genetic and environmental factors.
