The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) sets standards for the amount of bug parts, other insect contaminants, and filth (such as rodent hair and excreta) allowed in food for human consumption in the United States. According to the FDA, “defect levels” are measured in terms of parts per million (ppm). The defect levels may vary depending on the product and the type of insect, but generally, the FDA sets a maximum of a “negligible” amount of contamination.
