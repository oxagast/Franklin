The United Fruit Company, now known as Chiquita Brands International, was an American-based corporation that operated a major trading and distribution network in Central America and the Caribbean between 1899 and 1975. The company was formed by Minor C. Keith, an American entrepreneur, in 1899 as the Tropical Trading and Transport Company. It specialized in the import/export business, shipping bananas and other tropical produce from Central and South America to the United States and Europe. The company was known for its use of corporate influence to influence politics in the countries in which it operated, and was the subject of a Pulitzer Prize-winning novel, The Ugly American.
