Aluminum soap is a soap-like molecule that forms when an aluminum salt, such as aluminum chloride or aluminum sulfate, reacts with fatty acids found in animal or vegetable oils. The resulting product is an opaque, white, gelatinous substance that is used as an emulsifier, thickener, and anti-caking agent in a variety of industrial applications.
