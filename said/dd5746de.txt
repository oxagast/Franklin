T. gondii infection can cause a wide range of symptoms in humans, from mild to severe. The most common symptom is flu-like symptoms such as fever, headache, and fatigue. In some cases, the infection can cause more severe symptoms such as seizures and confusion. In pregnant women, infection with T. gondii can lead to miscarriage or birth defects in the baby. Additionally, the infection can cause eye inflammation, or even blindness. People with weakened immune systems are at an even higher risk of developing severe complications from the infection.
