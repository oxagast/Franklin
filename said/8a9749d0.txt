An animal control department is a government agency responsible for enforcing laws and regulations related to animal welfare and public safety. This may include responding to animal-related emergencies, investigating animal cruelty cases, and managing animal populations. A dog pound, on the other hand, is a facility that houses stray and abandoned dogs. It is usually operated by a local government or animal welfare organization and serves as a temporary shelter for these animals until they are adopted or reunited with their owners. While both aim to protect and care for animals, their roles and responsibilities differ.
