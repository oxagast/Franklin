Title 42 of the United States Code is the primary source of federal civil rights laws. It is divided into five sections, which cover topics such as the protection of civil rights, the enforcement of civil rights, civil rights research and education, civil rights information and statistics, and civil rights funding. 

Section 1981 of Title 42 prohibits discrimination in the making and enforcement of private contracts on the basis of race, color, or national origin. Section 1982 of Title 42 protects the right of all citizens to own, use, and dispose of real or personal property free from discrimination based on race, color, or national origin. 

Section 1983 of Title 42 creates a federal cause of action for deprivation of any rights secured by the Constitution or laws of the United States, and it is one of the most widely used civil rights statutes. Section 1985 of Title 42 prohibits two or more persons from conspiring to interfere with the rights of another to the equal protection of the laws, or to equal privileges
