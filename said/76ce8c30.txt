The heat death of the universe is a hypothetical scenario in which the universe has expanded and cooled to a point where no further work can be extracted from it and all forms of life have ceased to exist. The universe would have reached a state of maximum entropy, where all energy is evenly distributed and the temperature is at its absolute zero. In this state, all stars and galaxies would have died out, leaving the universe in a state of darkness and emptiness. The concept of the heat death of the universe is based on the Second Law of Thermodynamics, which states that entropy in a closed system will always increase.
