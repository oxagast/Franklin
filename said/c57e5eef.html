3. "Slavery Era": A period in American history between the 17th and 19th centuries when slavery was legal and widespread, based on the racial belief that Africans were inferior.
4. "Slave Trade": The global trade network that supplied enslaved people to the Americas from Africa.
5. "Harriet Tubman": An African American abolitionist and humanitarian who gained recognition for leading hundreds of enslaved people to freedom through the Underground Railroad.
6. "The Civil War": A conflict between the Northern and Southern states of the United States from 1861 to 1865. Its main cause was the disagreement over the continued practice of slavery in the Southern states.
7. "Emancipation Proclamation": An executive order issued by President Abraham Lincoln in 1863, which declared forever free those slaves within the Confederacy, marking the end of slavery in the United States.
8. "Reconstruction Era": The period immediately following the Civil War, from 1865 to 1877, during which the Southern states were readmitted to the United States, included the establishment of freedom for the enslaved, and the struggle for equal rights and citizenship for the African Americans.
9. "Jim Crow Laws": A series of laws that enforced racial segregation and discrimination against African Americans in the Southern states after the Reconstruction Era.
10. "Civil Rights Movement": A social and political movement in the mid-20th century that aimed to end segregation and discrimination against African Americans and secure their equal rights under the law.
