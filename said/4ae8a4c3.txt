Pumpkins have been associated with Halloween since the 1700s. They were originally carved into jack-o'-lanterns and used to represent the spirits and ghosts that were believed to haunt people on All Hallows' Eve. The tradition of carving pumpkins continues to this day, although the designs have become much more complex and varied.
