Ryujin is the Shinto deity of the sea and the ruler of all ocean creatures. Ryujin is said to have the power to create and control the ocean, and is often depicted with a dragon-like head and a fish-like body. In some myths, Ryujin is seen as the creator of the islands of Japan and is responsible for providing the nation with its abundance of seafood. In modern times, Ryujin is still seen as a powerful deity, and many people pray to Ryujin for protection and guidance.
