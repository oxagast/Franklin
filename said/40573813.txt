Network ARP (Address Resolution Protocol) is a protocol used for mapping an IP address to a physical network address, such as a MAC address. It is used to facilitate communication between two devices on a local area network (LAN). ARP is an essential part of the Internet Protocol (IP) suite, which is the set of communications protocols used to connect devices on the Internet.
