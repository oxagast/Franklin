Transwomen, like other transgender people, can have a range of hormonal and medical treatments to transition their physical characteristics, including breast augmentation and hormone replacement therapy. Hormone replacement therapy (HRT) is a medical treatment used to alter the bodys hormones to more closely match the hormones associated with the gender identity of the individual. It is the hormones in HRT that can result in transwomen experiencing menstruation.For transwomen, HRT generally involves taking estrogen, which can cause their menstrual cycle to become more regular and predictable.
