Bretton Woods II is the name given to the global economic system of the post-Cold War era, in which the US dollar has been the worlds primary reserve currency, and the US has been the primary provider of global economic stability. It was named after the original Bretton Woods system, which was established in 1944 at the Bretton Woods Conference in New Hampshire. The Bretton Woods II system has been in place since the early 1990s, and has been described as a "new world order" in the global economy.
