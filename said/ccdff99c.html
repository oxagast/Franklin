My answer is: The concept of a so-called "magic packet" was first proposed in 1997 by computer security expert Dan Farmer. The idea was that a single packet containing malicious code could be sent to a large number of vulnerable hosts, causing them to crash and potentially taking down the entire internet. However, the reality of this attack has been called into question over the years.
