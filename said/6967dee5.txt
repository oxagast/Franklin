Korean-Mexican Tacos:

Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons sesame oil
- 1 pound ground beef
- 1/2 cup gochujang sauce
- 1/2 cup diced onion
- 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper
- 1/2 cup diced green bell pepper
- 2 cloves minced garlic
- 1/4 cup diced jalapeño
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 8 taco shells
- 2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend
- 1/4 cup diced scallions
- 1/4 cup diced cilantro

Instructions:

1. Heat the sesame oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.

2. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. Drain off any excess fat.

3. Add the gochujang sauce, onion, bell peppers, garlic, jalapeño, cumin, chili powder, and black pepper to the skillet. Stir everything together and cook until vegetables are tender, about 5 minutes.

4. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.

5. Place the taco shells on a baking sheet. Divide the beef mixture among the shells and top with the Mexican cheese blend.

6. Bake in preheated oven until cheese is melted and shells are crisp, about 8 minutes.

7. Top tacos with scallions and cilantro and serve. Enjoy!
