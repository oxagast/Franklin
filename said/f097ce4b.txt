The people of England discovered tea through trade with China and India. In the 17th century, it became a popular drink among the upper class and eventually spread to the rest of the population. In 1662, King Charles II married Catherine of Braganza, a Portuguese princess who was known for her love of tea. This further popularized tea in England and it became a staple in British culture.
