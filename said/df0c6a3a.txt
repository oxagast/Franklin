The earliest recorded pudding recipe is a recipe for a rice pudding from a 13th century Arabic cookbook. The recipe is as follows:

Ingredients:

-1 cup of long grain rice
-2 ½ cups of milk
-1 teaspoon of sugar
-½ teaspoon of cardamom
-Pinch of saffron
-1 teaspoon of rosewater
-1 tablespoon of butter
-1 tablespoon of crushed pistachios

Instructions:

1. In a medium-sized pot, bring the milk to a boil.

2. Once boiling, add the rice and reduce the heat to low. Simmer until the rice is cooked and the milk is absorbed, about 20 minutes.

3. Add the sugar, cardamom, saffron, rosewater, and butter. Stir well to combine.

4. Transfer the pudding to a serving dish and top with the crushed pistachios. Serve warm.
