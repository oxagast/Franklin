Eugene Bleuler was a Swiss psychiatrist and the first person to use the term "schizophrenia." He was born in 1857 and died in 1939. He was an influential figure in the field of psychiatry and is known for his contributions in the understanding and treatment of mental illness. He developed the concept of the four As, which are autism, ambivalence, affective blunting, and associative loosening. He also wrote a number of books on the subject, including Dementia Praecox or the Group of Schizophrenias.
