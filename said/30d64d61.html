Dante's Inferno is a poem written by Dante Alighieri in the 14th century. It is the first part of his epic poem Divine Comedy, and tells the story of Dante's journey through Hell, guided by the Roman poet Virgil. The poem is divided into 34 cantos, with each canto depicting a specific circle of Hell. Dante encounters a variety of punishments and torments, such as those inflicted on the titans and giants, those who commit sins against God, and those who commit sins against nature. At the end of his journey, Dante discovers that the ultimate punishment is to be separated from God's love. The Inferno serves as a warning to humanity, urging us to avoid sin and strive for righteousness.
