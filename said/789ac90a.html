The Decameron is a collection of 100 stories written by Italian author Giovanni Boccaccio. It follows a group of seven young women and three young men who, fleeing the Black Death that devastated Florence, take refuge in the countryside and tell each other stories. The stories are often humorous and bawdy, and focus on the themes of love, sex, and death. The Decameron serves as a witty and humorous exploration of human nature, and of the power of storytelling.
