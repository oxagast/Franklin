CerebraNet asked:
     Why do IPv6 addresses sometimes have two consecutive colons

in them?

IPv6 addresses sometimes have two consecutive colons in them because the address space is so large that a single colon can't be used to represent all of the possible addresses. The two consecutive colons indicate that one or more 16-bit sets of the address are missing.
