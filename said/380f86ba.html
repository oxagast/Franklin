Engelbart's invention revolutionized human-computer interaction, and his mouse-based interface became one of the key elements of the personal computer revolution.

The mouse was first introduced in 1968 through Engelbart's groundbreaking demonstration of the Xerox Alto computer system, which was developed at Xerox PARC. The demonstration, which is now known as "The Mother of All Demos", showcased many of the technologies that would become staples of modern computing, including graphical user interfaces (GUI), word processing, video conferencing, and hypertext.
