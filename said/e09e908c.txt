Low blood pressure (hypotension) can be a sign of various conditions, including dehydration, pregnancy, certain medications, and diseases such as diabetes, heart disease, and anemia. It can also be a sign of an underlying medical condition, such as an endocrine disorder. If you have signs and symptoms of low blood pressure, it's important to seek medical care.
