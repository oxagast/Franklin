     Define research psychologist

A research psychologist is a type of psychologist who specializes in conducting research to better understand how the human mind works and how people behave. They use their research to inform decisions and understanding on a range of topics that are relevant to psychology, such as mental health, child development, learning, and decision-making. Research psychologists often work in academic settings, but may also be employed by private companies, government agencies, and non-profits.
