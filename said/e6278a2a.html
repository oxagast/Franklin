A Potemkin village (also Potyomkin village, derived from Russian: Потёмкинские деревни, Potyomkinskiye dereveni) is any construction built solely to deceive others into thinking that a situation is better than it really is. The term comes from stories of a fake portable village, built only to impress Empress Catherine II during her journey to Crimea in 1787.
